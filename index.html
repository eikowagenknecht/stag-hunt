▶ Strategy Explanations
Always Stag: Always attempts to hunt the stag. Maximizes cooperation but risky if the opponent hunts hare or if the stag doesn't appear.
Always Hare: Always hunts hare for guaranteed food. Safe and reliable (2 food when both hunt hare, 4 food when opponent hunts stag), but misses out on higher stag payoffs.
Tit-for-Tat: Starts by hunting stag, then mirrors the opponent's previous choice. Encourages cooperation while punishing defection.
Random: Randomly chooses stag or hare with 50% probability each round. Unpredictable and generally suboptimal.
Cautious: Adapts based on food reserves. Hunts hare when food is below the daily consumption threshold (playing it safe), otherwise hunts stag. Balances risk and reward.
